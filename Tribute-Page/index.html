Nelson Mandela

The man who ended apartheid

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 -Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1925 -Mandela begins primary school near his home village of Qunu.
  • 1930 -His father Mphakanyiswa Gadla Henry Mandela dies.
  • 1939 -Enrols at the University College of Fort Hare in Alice, Eastern Cape, for a bachelor of arts degree.
  • 1940 -Expelled from university for joining in a protest boycott.
  • 1941 -Meets Walter Sisulu, an active member of the African National Congress (ANC), South Africa’s major anti-apartheid liberation movement.
  • 1944 -Founds the ANC Youth League together with Anton Lembede, Oliver Tambo and Walter Sisulu.
  • 1948 -Mandela is elected national secretary of the ANC Youth League.
  • 1951 -Elected president of the ANC Youth League.
  • 1952 -Mandela is arrested and tried with Walter Sisulu and 18 others under the Supression of Communism Act for his role in the campaign. He is sentenced to nine months’ imprisonment with hard labour, suspended for two years.
  • 1960 -The South African government declares a state of emergency, detaining more than 18 000 people, and banning the ANC and other liberation movements
  • 1962 -Mandela is arrested near Howick in Natal and imprisoned in the Johannesburg Fort. He would only be free again in 1990, 28 years later.
  • 1990 -Mandela walks free from Pollsmoor Prison in Cape Town.
  • 1993 -Mandela is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his role in ending the apartheid.
  • 1994 -Mandela is inaugurated as South Africa’s first democratically elected president.
